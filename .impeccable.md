## Design Context

### Users

Hackathon attendees, primarily students, use the experience on mobile after scanning a booth QR code. They answer eight fast scenario questions, receive a meme-native persona, interact with its Pet, and may download the Pet package.

### Brand Personality

Playful, irreverent, and crafted. The experience should feel like a Chinese-internet meme translated into a confident hackathon identity, with Mosoo present but not intrusive.

### Aesthetic Direction

A bright retro print-shop ticket system: paper texture, hard ink lines, pixel typography, bold blue and orange accents, familiar stickers, and pixel Pets. Motion should feel responsive and deliberate—not glossy, elastic, or generically app-like.

### Design Principles

- Optimize the main flow for one-handed mobile use and short booth attention spans.
- Give every selection immediate visual acknowledgment before changing context.
- Use one coordinated transform-and-opacity transition at a time; avoid stacked CSS and JavaScript motion.
- Preserve the original familiar SVG artwork and the ticket-print visual grammar.
- Respect reduced-motion preferences and keep result/download actions fully usable without animation.
